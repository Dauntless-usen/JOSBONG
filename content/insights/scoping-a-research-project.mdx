Most research projects don't fail because the research was done badly.
They fail because the question was never pinned down in the first place,
so the output doesn't actually answer what the reader needed.

## Start with the decision, not the topic

Before opening a single source, ask what decision this research is meant
to inform. "Tell me about the market" is not a scope. "Should we enter
this market in the next two quarters" is.

## Define the deliverable format up front

A memo, a full report, and a dataset all require different research
processes. Agreeing on the format before starting avoids rework later.

## Set the boundary of "enough"

Research can always go deeper. Decide in advance what level of confidence
the decision actually requires, so you know when to stop.
