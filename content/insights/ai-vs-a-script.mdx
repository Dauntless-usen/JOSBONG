Not every automation problem needs a model. Reaching for AI when a
deterministic script would do adds cost, latency, and a new failure mode
without adding value.

## The gut-check

If the rule can be written down in a sentence and doesn't change based on
judgment or ambiguous input, it's a script. If the input is messy,
ambiguous, or the "correct" answer depends on context a human would
reason about, that's where a model earns its keep.

## Where teams get this wrong

The most common mistake is reaching for AI on the *first* pass, before
confirming a simpler rule-based approach can't get you 80% of the way
there for a fraction of the cost.
