Good research can still get rejected — or sent back for major revisions —
over structure rather than substance. A reviewer who has to work to find
your argument is a reviewer primed to find fault with it.

## Lead every section with its conclusion

State the point of a section in its first sentence. Let the evidence
follow, not precede, the claim.

## One idea per paragraph

If a paragraph is doing two jobs, split it. Reviewers skim, and a
paragraph that drifts between two claims usually gets credit for neither.

## Signal the structure explicitly

Phrases like "this section addresses..." feel unnecessary to the writer
and are exactly what a tired reviewer needs to stay oriented.
