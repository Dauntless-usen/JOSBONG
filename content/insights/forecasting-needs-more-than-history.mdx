A forecasting model trained purely on historical sales will faithfully
reproduce the past - including every stockout, every missed promotion,
and every event it never saw coming.

## Historical data encodes past mistakes

If demand was suppressed by a stockout, that gap looks like low demand to
a model that only sees historical sales, not the constraint that caused
it.

## External signals matter more than people expect

Weather, local events, and competitor promotions often explain more
forecast error than any amount of additional historical data ever will.

## Treat the first model as a baseline, not the answer

The value of a first forecasting pass is in showing you where the errors
cluster - which is usually exactly where to add the next signal.
